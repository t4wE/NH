Lakes can exhibit a wide range of colors, from crystal clear to murky brown, depending on factors such as sediment content, algae levels, and dissolved organic matter. Some lakes, like the famous Blue Lake in New Zealand, are known for their exceptionally clear and vibrant blue waters.
